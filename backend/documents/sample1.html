Understanding Post-Quantum Cryptography

Post-quantum cryptography (PQC) refers to cryptographic algorithms that are secure against attacks by quantum computers. This document explains the fundamentals of PQC.

Why Post-Quantum Cryptography?

Quantum computers have the potential to break many of the cryptographic systems currently in use. Shor's algorithm, for example, can factor large integers efficiently on a quantum computer, which would break RSA encryption.

Key Point: Post-quantum cryptography provides security guarantees that remain valid even when quantum computers become available.

NIST Post-Quantum Cryptography Standardization

The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) has been working to standardize post-quantum cryptographic algorithms. In 2024, NIST selected several algorithms including:

  • CRYSTALS-Kyber - Key encapsulation mechanism
  • CRYSTALS-Dilithium - Digital signatures
  • FALCON - Digital signatures
  • SPHINCS+ - Digital signatures

Implementation in This Project

This messaging application uses CRYSTALS-Kyber for post-quantum key exchange, ensuring that communications remain secure against quantum attacks.
